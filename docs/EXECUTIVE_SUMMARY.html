2. Market Context: Syria Healthcare

Infrastructure Crisis: Years of conflict have severely damaged Syria's healthcare system. Nearly 40% of healthcare facilities have been destroyed, and only 57% of hospitals remain fully operational. An estimated 50-70% of healthcare workers have left the country. [1][2][3]

Opportunity: The new Syrian government has declared healthcare reconstruction a national priority. Digital health solutions are essential to efficiently connect patients with available providers and maximize the impact of limited medical resources. [5][6]

3. Problem Statement

Current healthcare access in Syria faces significant barriers:

  • No centralized system to find available doctors or book appointments
  • Patients rely on phone calls during limited hours, often facing long wait times
  • Medical records are fragmented and frequently lost due to displacement
  • No secure channel for patient-doctor communication
  • Managing healthcare for family members requires multiple separate processes

4. Solution: DocSera Platform

DocSera provides a comprehensive mobile platform that enables patients to discover doctors, book appointments in real-time, communicate securely, and manage health records for themselves and their families. The platform operates 24/7 and is designed for the Syrian context with full Arabic language support.

7. Technology Overview

DocSera is built on a modern, scalable technology stack designed for reliability in challenging infrastructure environments:

  • Frontend: Flutter (cross-platform iOS/Android)
  • Backend: Supabase (PostgreSQL, Authentication, Real-time)
  • Security: Row-Level Security, encrypted data storage
  • Notifications: Push notifications for appointments and messages
